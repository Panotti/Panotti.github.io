Echo ... echooo ... can you hear me?
You dragged me through the mud of our best experiences. I have been exposed to glass for waaaay too long. Nightsweat; I realise that all I'm looking for is a bunch of backs pressed against mine, preventing me from stumbling over a succession of wrong thoughts. Of the type that make you think you're right and you think right and so on and so on ... But good, you were there, said this way out of the yard, broke some branches on the way, ripped a seam, I enjoyed it.

Talked to you on the phone the other day—WhatsApp voice call—and I noticed the dissecting and unsettling silence fading in between each spoken word, like someone holding the breath or stopping time. Or a tap closed between each filling of a glass. It was a clean, cleansed, silence—one purified by the dullness of calculation, of automation, of regulation and optimisation.

The feeling of talking with friends via landline connection—it felt like a direct channel opened up between our ears and tongues, an airiness of circulation.
I could hear the other person breathing and the slight static noise of electricity hardwiring us.

Now I can't hear the other person's breath anymore. The algorithmic sound detection for controlling the threshold of the microphone transmission seems to not register breath as a signal deemed necessary for communication.
